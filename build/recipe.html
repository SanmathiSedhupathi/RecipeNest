Blackberry Slump

Sugar 2 tablespoons Buy Now

Sugar 2 tablespoons Buy Now

  1. Make the dumplings:

    In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Cut the butter into small cubes and add to the flour. Using a pastry cutter, two knives, or your clean hands, cut the butter into the flour until the flour resembles a coarse meal. Add the milk all at once and stir until the flour is just moistened. Handling the dough as little as possible, form into a shaggy ball. Set aside.

  2. Make the filling:

    In a 2-quart saucepan or 10-inch wide and at least 2-inch deep skillet (with a tight-fitting lid), add the berries, sugar, cinnamon, lemon zest, lemon juice, and water. Heat until boiling, stirring a few times so that the berries are well coated with the sauce.

  3. Add the dumpling dough:

    Once the berry mixture is boiling, tear off spoonful chunks from the dough ball and drop onto the fruit around the edges of the pot. You should have enough dough for 6 dumplings.

  4. Cover and simmer:

    Cover the pot and reduce the heat to low to maintain a simmer. Cook for 25 minutes without peeking at the dumplings. Place the dumplings in serving bowls and top with the berries. Serve with cream or ice cream. Serve hot or chilled.


Apple Pie

Apples 6 cups Buy Now

  1. Prepare the Crust:

    In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Cut in butter until mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Stir in water, a tablespoon at a time, until mixture forms a ball. Divide dough in half, shape into balls, and wrap in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.

  2. Prepare the Filling:

    In a large bowl, combine sliced apples, sugar, flour, cinnamon, and salt. Mix until apples are evenly coated.

  3. Roll Out the Dough:

    Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Roll out one ball of dough on a lightly floured surface to fit a 9-inch pie plate. Transfer dough to pie plate. Trim even with edge of plate. Add apple mixture and dot with butter.

  4. Top with Second Crust:

    Roll out remaining dough to fit top of pie. Place over filling. Trim, seal, and flute edges. Cut slits in dough to allow steam to escape.

  5. Bake the Pie:

    Cover edges loosely with foil. Bake in preheated oven for 50 minutes. Remove foil and bake until crust is golden brown, about 20 minutes more. Cool on a wire rack.

Tomato Basil Soup

  •  All Seasons

  1. Sauté the Aromatics:

    In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and chopped onion. Sauté until the onion is translucent.

  2. Add the Tomatoes:

    Stir in the diced tomatoes and cook for about 5 minutes, until they start to break down.

  3. Add the Broth:

    Pour in the vegetable broth and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for 15 minutes.

  4. Blend the Soup:

    Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. Alternatively, transfer the soup to a blender in batches and blend until smooth. Return the soup to the pot.

  5. Finish the Soup:

    Stir in the fresh basil leaves and heavy cream. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Let the soup simmer for another 5 minutes to blend the flavors.

  6. Serve:

    Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with additional basil leaves and a drizzle of olive oil if desired. Serve hot.
